# How to Load the 300+ Vocabulary Words

## Quick Fix: Two Easy Methods

### Method 1: Use the Green Banner (Easiest!)

1. Open the app: http://localhost:5173
2. You should see a **green banner** at the top that says "300+ B1 Vocabulary Words Available!"
3. Click the **"Load 300+ Words Now"** button
4. Confirm when asked
5. The app will reload with all 300+ words! ✅

### Method 2: Use the Reset Button

1. Open the app: http://localhost:5173
2. Click the **"Manage"** tab at the top
3. Click the **"🔄 Reset"** button (orange button)
4. Confirm when asked
5. All 300+ vocabulary words will be loaded! ✅

### Method 3: Clear Browser Data (Manual)

If the above don't work, you can manually clear the cache:

**In Chrome/Edge:**
1. Press `F12` to open Developer Tools
2. Go to the **Application** tab
3. Click **Local Storage** on the left
4. Click `http://localhost:5173`
5. Right-click and select **Clear**
6. Close Developer Tools
7. Refresh the page (`Ctrl+R` or `Cmd+R`)

**In Firefox:**
1. Press `F12` to open Developer Tools
2. Go to the **Storage** tab
3. Click **Local Storage**
4. Click `http://localhost:5173`
5. Right-click and select **Delete All**
6. Close Developer Tools
7. Refresh the page (`Ctrl+R` or `Cmd+R`)

**In Safari:**
1. Open Safari Preferences → Advanced
2. Enable "Show Develop menu"
3. Go to Develop → Show Web Inspector
4. Go to Storage tab
5. Select Local Storage → http://localhost:5173
6. Clear all items
7. Refresh the page

## Verify It Worked

After using any method above:

1. Go to the **Stats** tab
2. You should see **300+** Total Cards (not just 3!)
3. Go to the **Manage** tab
4. Scroll down to see hundreds of vocabulary cards listed

## Why This Happened

You opened the app before I added the 300+ words, so your browser saved the old 3-card sample data. The app respects your saved data and doesn't overwrite it automatically (to protect your progress).

That's why you need to manually reset it to load the new vocabulary!
